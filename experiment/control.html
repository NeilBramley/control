The study should not take longer than 10 minutes. Upon completion of the study, you will be paid a basic fee of $ 0.5 and an additional bonus between 0 and $ 0.5 depending on your sailing performance.

Instructions:

Please read the following instructions very carefully:


In the following study, it is your task to sail a boat as well as you can. For this you have to aim for a direction such that you end up at points on which you will receive high evaluation points.

You will see a graph just like the one on the next page. You can then determine a value of the input that you want to investigate. After you have made a choice of input value, you will see the corresponding output value. The output value is determined by the underlying relation and added to the graph.

You have a total amount of 40 observations available. That means you have 40 chances to enter an input and observe the output for that input. It is your task to choose the input wisely, so that you get a good feeling for which output is produced at different input points.

Even though inputs are direclty related to outputs, there is some variation in the ouputs produced by an input value. This means that observed output points for some inputs can vary slightly and are not always the same. It is your task to determine how much they can vary. The way they vary is the same across all points, which means that you will not encounter big deviations for one input value and small deviations at others.

After the learning task, you will be confronted with a short test to check how well you have learned the relations between inputs and outputs. Your reward will be based on your performance in the test.

For every task, you will see a page with the instructions, an input slider, and the graph. You can hide the instructions by clicking "Hide explanations". You can show them by pressing "Show explanations".

Explanation:


I. The green dot marks the direction you are aiming for at each round. When you move the slider, the green dot will move around as well.

II. When you click on "GO" the value of the green dot at that moment will be used as an input and you will sail in that direction. However, the sea has an underlying current, so you will normally not end up at exactly the place you were aiming for.

III. You get evaluated at each round by how close you are to the dark blue bits. The darker the blue, the more points you will get. The redder, the less points you will get. You can get a maximum of 100 points per round.

IV. On top of the graph, you can see how many points you got at the current round and how many points you have gotten in total so far. Your payment later on will depend on the total score.

V.Remember, it is your task to sail the boat to the dark blue parts at each evaluation round. For this, you have to learn the underlying current of the sea and aim for the right direction in order to hit the good marks at each evaluation round.
